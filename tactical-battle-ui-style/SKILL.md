# Tactical Battle UI Style

## Overview

Use this skill to create grounded tactical shooter interfaces that feel clear, tense, and functional. The style may be inspired by modern battle royale shooters, but must remain original and avoid copying protected game layouts, icons, maps, fonts, or weapon art.

## Visual Direction

- Mood: tactical, grounded, high-alert, survival-focused, military-adjacent, clean under pressure.
- Palette: charcoal, slate, muted olive, sand, off-white, warning amber, danger red, team cyan/green.
- Use high contrast for combat-critical information and lower contrast for secondary panels.
- Prefer thin lines, compact panels, transparent dark overlays, crisp icons, and restrained animation.
- Keep decorative elements minimal. Clarity beats style during combat.

## HUD Components

- Compass: top-center heading, degree marks, ping indicators, shot direction if supported.
- Minimap: safe zone edge, teammate markers, pings, terrain hints, and current heading.
- Weapon panel: current weapon, ammo in mag, reserve ammo, fire mode, attachments, throwable.
- Health and armor: readable bars with healing/boost progress and damaged states.
- Team panel: teammate health, downed state, distance, voice/activity, marker color.
- Kill feed: compact and non-blocking, with weapon or damage-type icons if available.
- Zone warning: countdown, damage threat, direction to safe area, and urgency escalation.
- Interaction prompt: pickup, revive, open, drive, reload, vault, swap, heal, cancel.

## Sniper And Scope UI

- Scope overlay must leave target area readable.
- Reticle should match actual hit or projectile rules.
- Show ammo, zeroing/range cue if supported, breath hold, and scope state with minimal clutter.
- Do not use exact reticles or scope art from a specific game.

## Inventory And Loot UI

- Keep looting fast: nearby list, item category, rarity, compatibility, pickup action.
- Show attachment compatibility clearly.
- Use compare states for armor, backpack, scope, and weapon attachments.
- Avoid large modal inventory during active combat unless the genre expects it.

## Mobile And Desktop

- Desktop: support keyboard/mouse readability, keybind hints, and precise cursor interactions.
- Mobile: large thumb targets, separated fire/aim/crouch/jump/reload/heal buttons, customizable layout if possible.
- Keep text and icons readable over bright outdoor terrain and dark interiors.

## Motion And Feedback

- Use fast but subtle feedback: hit marker, armor break, downed, eliminated, low health, low ammo, zone damage, teammate down.
- Avoid excessive red flashes, screen shake, or opaque warnings that hide enemies.
- Damage indicators should show direction without blocking aim.

## Avoid

- Copying exact battle royale HUD layouts, map visuals, icons, or typography from existing games.
- Dense UI that blocks sightlines.
- Low-contrast ammo, health, or zone warnings.
- Decorative military clutter that slows scanning.
- Scope overlays that do not match actual gameplay aim.

## Acceptance Checklist

- Combat-critical HUD information is readable within a glance.
- Minimap, compass, zone, team state, health, armor, weapon, and ammo are clear.
- Loot and inventory UI supports fast decisions.
- Sniper/scope UI stays accurate and uncluttered.
- Mobile and desktop layouts avoid overlap and preserve aim visibility.
- The style is original while matching the tactical survival shooter mood.
