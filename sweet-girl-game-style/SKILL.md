# Sweet Girl Game Style

## Overview

Use this skill to create game interfaces that feel sweet, bright, soft, and polished. Build the style around pink, yellow, and white, with rounded forms, cute icons, gentle highlights, and clear readable UI.

## Style Direction

- Aim for sweet and elegant rather than cluttered. The UI can be cute, but it must still feel clean and playable.
- Use white and warm off-white as breathing space, pink for identity and primary actions, and yellow for rewards, warmth, highlights, and joy.
- Keep contrast readable. Pale pink text on white is usually too weak; use rose, plum, cocoa, or warm gray for text.
- Use soft shapes: rounded panels, pill buttons, scallop edges, bow-like dividers, heart badges, star sparkles, ribbon tabs, sticker-style icons, and soft shadow.
- Make decorative elements small and intentional. Let spacing, hierarchy, and color carry the look.
- Favor a gentle, cheerful mood suitable for casual games, dress-up games, collection games, character games, cozy simulations, and visual-novel style screens.

## Palette

Use pink, yellow, and white as the core system, then add one grounding text color and one optional cool accent.

Recommended base palette:

- Background: `#FFF9FB`, `#FFFDF7`, `#FFFFFF`
- Soft pink: `#FFD6E7`, `#FFB8D2`, `#FF8FBE`
- Strong pink: `#F45F9B`, `#D94F86`
- Cream yellow: `#FFF1B8`, `#FFE38A`, `#FFC94A`
- Text: `#4A3541`, `#6D5260`, `#7A6A72`
- Borders and shadows: `#F3C7D8`, `#F7DDE8`, `rgba(212, 111, 150, 0.18)`
- Optional fresh accent: `#8EDDD2` or `#A7D8FF`

Suggested ratio: 55-65% white/off-white, 20-30% pink, 10-15% yellow, 5% accent or dark text. Avoid filling the entire screen with saturated pink.

## Visual Elements

### Shapes

- Use rounded rectangles with 12-20px radius for main panels and buttons.
- Use pill chips for currencies, hearts, stamina, gems, tickets, and event points.
- Use scalloped or ribbon-like accents for headers, tabs, reward banners, and event labels.
- Use sticker cards with a thin pastel border and a soft shadow for characters, outfits, items, and quests.
- Use hearts, stars, bows, flowers, sparkles, candies, envelopes, crowns, and small plush-like icons only when they match the game theme.

### Texture And Effects

- Use subtle gradients from white to blush or cream, not heavy neon gradients.
- Use soft shadows below panels and buttons, with low opacity and warm pink tint.
- Use tiny sparkles, dots, lace-like dividers, or diagonal stripe patterns as accents, not full-screen noise.
- Use glossy highlights sparingly on primary buttons, reward items, and rare cards.
- Make reward moments feel special with a yellow glow, star pop, confetti, or gentle bounce.

## UI Components

- Primary button: saturated pink fill, white label, soft shadow, small icon, pressed state that lowers the button.
- Secondary button: white or cream fill, pink border, pink text, clear hover and focus state.
- Reward button: yellow fill, rose or cocoa text, star icon, slightly brighter highlight.
- HUD chip: white fill, pink outline, icon plus number, compact but readable.
- Navigation tabs: rounded or ribbon tabs with active pink fill and inactive white fill.
- Dialog: white panel, pink title strip, rounded corners, warm shadow, clear primary action.
- Inventory or shop card: white card, pastel border, item art, rarity ribbon, price chip, and selected outline.
- Character card: larger art area, soft blush background, name plaque, mood or rarity badge.
- Progress bar: cream track with pink or yellow fill, rounded ends, optional heart/star markers.

## Typography

- Use rounded sans-serif fonts for UI. Use a decorative cute display font only for titles or event banners.
- Keep body text clear and medium weight. Do not use thin pastel text.
- Use short labels for buttons and chips. Long copy belongs in dialogs or story panels.
- Keep hierarchy simple: screen title, section title, item label, description, number chip.

## Layout

- Keep the main character, outfit, room, board, or reward visually central when the screen is character-driven.
- Put currency, stamina, hearts, and settings in a tidy top bar.
- Put high-frequency actions in a bottom bar or a clear right-side action stack on desktop.
- Use generous spacing between cards. A sweet style looks worse when every surface is crowded.
- Keep mobile controls thumb-friendly with at least 44px hit targets.
- Avoid nesting cards inside cards. Use a main panel with grouped rows, tabs, or separate repeated item cards.

## Motion

- Use gentle easing, small bounce, shimmer, pulse, and reward pop effects.
- Keep idle animation subtle: floating stars, blinking badges, breathing character highlight, or slow sparkle drift.
- Add tactile feedback to buttons: hover lift, press dip, selected glow.
- Avoid aggressive screen shake, harsh flashes, or fast repeated animation.

## Avoid

- Overusing saturated hot pink across the whole screen.
- Low-contrast pale pink labels on white or yellow.
- Too many bows, hearts, sparkles, stickers, and borders at once.
- Adult glamour, sharp luxury fashion, or dark romantic styling unless the user asks for that direction.
- Generic corporate UI. This should feel like a game, with collectible, reward, character, or playful cues.
- Childish clutter when the target should feel polished and pretty.

## Workflow

1. Identify the game screen type: main menu, HUD, dress-up, shop, gacha, character profile, room decoration, inventory, level select, reward, settings, or story.
2. Choose the core white-pink-yellow palette and one readable dark text color.
3. Decide the emotional emphasis: candy cute, princess, idol, cozy room, magical girl, romantic diary, fashion boutique, or pet care.
4. Define reusable components: buttons, chips, cards, tabs, dialogs, rewards, badges, and progress elements.
5. Apply sweetness through color, shape, icons, and spacing before adding decoration.
6. Check responsive layouts on mobile and desktop for clipped text, crowded cards, and weak contrast.
7. Polish with small motion and reward effects where they support gameplay.

## Acceptance Checklist

- The interface clearly reads as sweet, feminine, soft, and game-like.
- Pink, yellow, and white are the dominant colors, with enough contrast for all labels and numbers.
- Decorations support the screen instead of overwhelming it.
- Buttons, cards, chips, dialogs, tabs, and rewards share a consistent soft rounded treatment.
- The main action and game state are clear within the first glance.
- Mobile and desktop layouts have no overlapping, clipped, or unreadable text.
